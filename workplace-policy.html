Workplace, Conduct, Working Hours & Probation Policy

Effective: Jan 2026
1. Purpose

This Policy sets out the minimum standards and requirements governing workplace conduct, working hours, attendance, probation, employee responsibilities, and operational expectations at Shikisha Tours & Travel (‚Äúthe Company‚Äù). It is intended to promote service excellence, fairness, employee wellbeing, and compliance with applicable laws and regulatory requirements.

2. Scope and Applicability

This Policy applies to:

  • All employees (full-time, part-time, casual, temporary);
  • Interns, trainees, and consultants where applicable; and
  • Any individual performing work on behalf of the Company.

This Policy applies at:

  • Company offices and facilities;
  • Client sites and partner locations;
  • During tours, transfers, field operations, and work travel; and
  • Company events and work-related communications.
3. Workplace Structure and On-site Requirement

Shikisha Tours & Travel operates as an on-site workplace to support travel operations, booking coordination, real-time service delivery, and effective collaboration.

Remote work is not standard practice and may only be permitted in exceptional circumstances with written approval from management.
4. Working Hours, Attendance & Operational Flexibility

The Company operates between 9:00 AM and 7:00 PM, Monday to Saturday, depending on workload, client demand, tour schedules, and operational requirements.

  • Specific reporting and shift arrangements may vary depending on operational workload.
  • Supervisors may assign rosters or schedule adjustments to ensure service continuity and fairness.
  • Employees must report on time and remain available for assigned duties.
  • Delays, absences, or emergencies must be communicated to a supervisor as early as possible.

Employees are entitled to reasonable breaks during the workday. Supervisors are required to manage workloads to support safe performance of duties and employee wellbeing.

5. Probation Policy

All new employees may be subject to a probation period as specified in their employment contract. Probation is intended to assess performance, reliability, service standards, and compliance with Company rules.

  • Supervisors will monitor performance throughout probation and may conduct formal reviews.
  • Employees are expected to demonstrate satisfactory performance, professionalism, and compliance.

At the end of probation, the Company may:

  • Confirm the employee in their role;
  • Extend probation where further assessment is required (subject to applicable law and contract terms); or
  • Terminate employment where performance and/or conduct is unsatisfactory, in accordance with applicable law and employment terms.
6. Code of Conduct

All employees must uphold high standards of professionalism, integrity, and client service. Employees must:

  • Treat clients, colleagues, and partners with respect and courtesy;
  • Avoid abusive, discriminatory, or harassing behaviour of any kind;
  • Protect Company and client confidentiality (including booking and personal data);
  • Disclose any conflict of interest to management;
  • Use Company property and resources responsibly for business purposes; and
  • Not report to work under the influence of alcohol or illegal substances.
Breaches of the Code of Conduct may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
7. Sick Leave & Illness Reporting

Shikisha Tours & Travel encourages employees to prioritize their health and the safety of colleagues and clients.

  • Employees are free to report when they are unwell and may request leave to recover.
  • Paid sick leave is provided, and employees will not be deducted salary for approved sick leave.
  • Where reasonably required, medical documentation may be requested for extended illness periods.
8. Workplace Benefits and Support
  • Collaborative Working Space to support teamwork and shared problem-solving.
  • Dedicated Focus Hours to support deep, distraction-free work where feasible.
  • Meals / Meal Support to keep our team energized throughout the day (where applicable).

10. Reporting Concerns & Discipline

Employees are encouraged to report misconduct, policy violations, harassment, or safety concerns to HR or management. The Company prohibits retaliation against employees who report concerns in good faith.

Failure to comply with this Policy may result in disciplinary action, including verbal warning, written warning, final warning, suspension (where lawful), or termination.

Policy Updates: This Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect operational needs, legal requirements, and changes in national health and safety guidelines. Employees will be notified of material updates through official communication channels.

Health, Safety & Vaccination Policy

Safety first
1. Commitment to Safety

The safety and wellbeing of our employees, clients, and partners is a top priority at Shikisha Tours & Travel. We follow national health guidelines and industry best practices to maintain a safe workplace and reliable operations.

2. General Workplace Safety
  • Regular sanitization of shared spaces and equipment
  • Availability of hygiene supplies, including hand sanitizers
  • Clear procedures for reporting illness, exposure, hazards, or incidents
  • Flexible and supportive sick leave arrangements to support recovery
3. Employee Health Support

Employees who feel unwell should report promptly to their supervisor and may take leave to recover. The Company encourages employees to seek medical attention when necessary and to return to work only when fit.

  • Paid sick leave applies when sick leave is appropriately communicated and approved.
  • Any workplace injury or incident must be reported immediately for proper support and documentation.
4. COVID-19 Vaccination Policy

Vaccination is recommended for all employees who work on-site. Staying up to date with COVID-19 vaccines helps us promote safer in-person interactions, reduce health-related service disruptions, and maintain continuity in travel operations.

  • Promote safer in-person interactions
  • Reduce health-related service disruptions
  • Maintain continuity in travel operations

Employees may be asked to present proof of vaccination only when required by government or health authorities.

Vaccine recommended for in-person work
5. Social Health Authority (SHA) Contributions

The Company complies with statutory healthcare requirements. Employee healthcare contributions are deducted in accordance with Social Health Authority (SHA) guidelines and applicable statutory caps and are remitted for employee access and use.

For more information about SHA, visit: sha.go.ke
